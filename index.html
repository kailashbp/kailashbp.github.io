The interlab is a series of studies (https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0150182, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0199432, https://doi.org/10.1038/s42003-020-01127-5) organized by the iGEM competition. It is one of the competition requirements to earn a bronze medal. The studies are aimed at addressing the problems frequenting reproducibility in science. The 2018 edition of the study aimed to study the interlab variability in fluorescent measurements. A widely used and important technique to measure the optical density (OD) of bacterial growth is based on fluorescent measurements. So, it is essential to address the variability in these measurements to ensure reproducibility of experiments. Team iGEM IIT-Madras was one of the teams that contributed experimental data to this study (http://2018.igem.org/Team:IIT-Madras/InterLab). The observations based on 244 different labs across the world that participated in the competition is detailed in the paper. IIT-Madras was also one of the 17 teams that provided usable flow cytometry data.
